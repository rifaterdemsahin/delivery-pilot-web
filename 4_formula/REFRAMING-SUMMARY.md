## The Core Problem Identified

### Current (Misaligned) Positioning
**What we say:** "We close AI skills gaps through training and workshops"  
**What we actually deliver:** "We build purpose-built AI agents that augment expert practitioners in complex delivery environments"

**The gap:** These are fundamentally different products with different customers, competitors, and value propositions.

## Root Cause Analysis Summary

### ROOT CAUSE 1: Product-Market Fit Confusion
- Marketing emphasizes "RAISE: Rapid AI Increases Skills Expectations"
- Solution presented as education (workshops, courses, training)
- BUT actual delivery: custom AI agents, workflow integration, knowledge capture
- **Fix:** Position as cognitive infrastructure, not education

### ROOT CAUSE 2: Target Customer Mismatch
- Marketing to: Organizations worried about AI skills gaps (L&D buyers)
- Should target: Platform teams drowning in infrastructure complexity (engineering leaders)
- **Fix:** Reframe pain point as "expertise scalability" not "skills gap"

### ROOT CAUSE 3: Feature vs. Benefit Confusion
- Features built: AI agents, workflow integration, knowledge codification
- Benefits marketed: "Skills development", "training programs"
- Should market: "10x engineer effectiveness", "scale without hiring"
- **Fix:** Lead with outcome (delivery velocity) not method (training)

### ROOT CAUSE 4: Wrong Success Metric
- Current implied metric: "Did people learn AI skills?"
- Actual metric that matters: "Did delivery velocity increase while maintaining reliability?"
- **Fix:** Measure operational KPIs, not educational outcomes
